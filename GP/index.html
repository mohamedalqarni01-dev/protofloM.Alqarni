CHAPTER 5 — GEOMETRY OPTIMIZATION
How the V-shape was selected — using the Global Performance Index

Three geometries were simulated in COMSOL Multiphysics across Ra = 10³ to 10⁶. Performance was evaluated using GPI — a combined score of Nusselt number (heat transfer) and entropy generation (efficiency losses).

Pyramid
Best GPI: 0.506 (L=0.7, Ra=10⁴)
Strong at large L values
Higher entropy at small L
SELECTED ✓
V-Shape
Best GPI: 0.560 (L=0.2, Ra=10⁶)
Highest Nu at moderate Ra
Fabricated at L=0.3 (2nd best)
Chimney
Best GPI: 0.437 (L=0.7, Ra=10⁴)
Consistent but lower Nu
Less sensitive to L changes
⚠️ Why L=0.3 and not L=0.2? The optimal GPI was at L=0.2 — but fabrication constraints (sealing difficulty, structural instability, workshop tool limitations) made it impractical. L=0.3 was the second-best manufacturable option and was used for the prototype.
CHAPTER 6 — STRUCTURAL FEA
Glass thickness selection — SolidWorks Simulation

Three glass cover thicknesses were analyzed under gravity loading. The allowable von Mises stress limit was set at 8 MPa (CF5 constraint). All three passed — but 6mm offered the best balance.

4 mm
σᵥₘ max: 5.03 MPa
Reaction: 448.6 N
Strain: 3.15×10⁻⁵
✓ Safe but prone to edge chipping during fabrication
SELECTED
6 mm
σᵥₘ max: 8.39 MPa
Reaction: 478.9 N
Strain: 1.90×10⁻⁴
✓ Best balance — robust, manufacturable, within limit
8 mm
σᵥₘ max: 8.37 MPa
Reaction: 520.1 N
Strain: 1.89×10⁻⁴
✓ Safe but overdesigned — unnecessary mass, no benefit over 6mm
